# Homepage Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines the Velora Studio homepage as a product and UX system. It is the source of truth for what each section must accomplish, why it exists, how it supports the user journey, and where the current structure should be challenged before future implementation continues.

The homepage must help a potential client understand one central idea: Velora Studio builds websites around the reason a customer should choose the business, not around generic templates or decoration. Every section should either clarify that positioning, reduce buying friction, prove capability, or move the visitor toward a conversation.

## Intended User Journey

The current journey is:

1. Establish the core positioning and primary action.
2. Frame the strategic question behind the work.
3. Name the problems that make most websites underperform.
4. Explain the studio method.
5. Present service categories.
6. Demonstrate story-led scrolling as a distinctive capability.
7. Explain the work process.
8. Prove capability through projects.
9. Introduce the studio and founder.
10. Resolve objections through FAQ.
11. Convert with a final call to action.

This is a strong strategic narrative, but it is longer than many studio homepages. The structure should be held to a strict standard: if a section repeats an earlier argument without creating new confidence, it should be merged or removed.

## Section: Header

### Primary Goal

Provide persistent orientation, brand recognition, navigation, and a direct contact path.

### User Question It Answers

"Where am I, and how do I quickly reach the part I care about?"

### Business Goal

Increase access to high-intent sections and keep the contact action available throughout the visit.

### Why This Section Exists

The homepage is long and narrative-driven. A sticky header prevents the user from feeling trapped inside a scrolling story and gives them control.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

None. It frames the entire page.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No. It is a global navigation layer, not content.

## Section: Hero

### Primary Goal

Communicate the studio's positioning immediately and give the visitor a clear next action.

### User Question It Answers

"What does this studio do, and why should I keep reading?"

### Business Goal

Qualify the right visitor and drive immediate WhatsApp inquiries from users who already resonate with the offer.

### Why This Section Exists

The hero owns the first impression. It must make the strategic premise unmistakable: the work starts from the reason customers choose the client, then becomes website structure, copy, design, and development.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends only on the header for navigation and contact continuity.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No. It is the homepage's positioning anchor.

## Section: Decision Board

### Primary Goal

Translate the hero promise into a simple decision framework: message, trust, proof, action.

### User Question It Answers

"What does 'built around the reason to choose me' actually mean?"

### Business Goal

Differentiate Velora from design-only or development-only providers by showing a strategic lens before services are discussed.

### Why This Section Exists

The hero makes a claim. This section operationalizes it into a memorable model. It makes the offer feel intentional rather than aesthetic.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Requires the hero to introduce the choosing/decision premise. Without the hero, the framework would feel abstract.

### Priority

Essential, if concise.

### Could It Be Merged?

Potentially. It could merge with the Approach section if the homepage needs to be shorter. The current Decision Board is a conceptual framework, while Approach is a method. They are related but not identical.

## Section: Problem

### Primary Goal

Name the failure modes of underperforming websites.

### User Question It Answers

"Why might my current site not be producing results even if it looks fine?"

### Business Goal

Create problem awareness and increase urgency for a strategy-led website.

### Why This Section Exists

Visitors often believe website performance is a visual problem. This section reframes the problem as clarity, trust, proof, and action.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on the Decision Board or hero to establish the central decision-making idea. It deepens the argument by showing what happens when that idea is missing.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

Potentially with Decision Board. The current Decision Board defines the positive model; Problem defines the negative symptoms. They could become one "Why websites fail / what must be fixed" section if page length becomes a concern.

## Section: Approach

### Primary Goal

Explain the studio's working philosophy before listing outputs.

### User Question It Answers

"How do you think before you build?"

### Business Goal

Position Velora as a strategic partner, not a vendor executing a template.

### Why This Section Exists

It bridges the problem diagnosis and the service offering. It tells the user the studio has a repeatable reasoning process.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on the Problem section to create the need for a method. It should feel like the answer to the problems just named.

### Priority

Essential, but vulnerable to redundancy.

### Could It Be Merged?

Yes. It overlaps with Decision Board and Process. If retained, it must stay focused on philosophy and decision logic, while Process stays focused on project execution.

## Section: Services

### Primary Goal

Clarify what the user can buy or inquire about.

### User Question It Answers

"What kind of website or digital experience can you build for me?"

### Business Goal

Segment demand into service families and help visitors self-identify.

### Why This Section Exists

After the strategic premise is clear, the user needs concrete offerings. This section turns the philosophy into available engagement types.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Approach to establish that services are selected based on business goal, audience, and message rather than format alone.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No, but it can absorb parts of Story Scrolling if that capability is treated as one service rather than a standalone narrative feature.

## Section: Story Scrolling

### Primary Goal

Demonstrate a distinctive capability: using scroll behavior to unfold complex messages gradually.

### User Question It Answers

"What makes a Velora site feel different from a normal static page?"

### Business Goal

Differentiate the studio, justify premium perception, and attract clients who value narrative digital experiences.

### Why This Section Exists

Story Scrolling is a named capability in the current service language. A live conceptual demonstration makes the idea more tangible than a service card alone.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Services introducing Story Scrolling as a service family or capability. It should feel like a zoom-in on one of the offerings, not a separate unrelated chapter.

### Priority

Nice to have for differentiation. Essential only if Story Scrolling is a strategic flagship offer.

### Could It Be Merged?

Yes. It could merge into Services as an expanded featured service, or into Work if shown through project examples. If page length or performance becomes an issue, this is the first large experiential section to reconsider.

## Section: Process

### Primary Goal

Explain how collaboration works from discovery to launch.

### User Question It Answers

"What happens if I decide to work with you?"

### Business Goal

Reduce uncertainty, make the service feel professional, and lower contact friction.

### Why This Section Exists

A high-trust service needs an understandable process. This section turns a creative service into a manageable engagement.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Services to make the user interested in an engagement type. It should answer operational concerns after desire has been created.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

Partially. It can borrow less from Approach. The Process section should not repeat philosophy; it should clarify collaboration, sequence, and expectations.

## Section: Work

### Primary Goal

Provide proof through real projects or representative case studies.

### User Question It Answers

"Have you done work that looks credible and relevant?"

### Business Goal

Increase trust, demonstrate range, and give users evidence before contacting.

### Why This Section Exists

The page makes many strategic claims. Work examples convert those claims into credibility.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Services and Process to give the user criteria for evaluating the examples. The work should prove strategy, clarity, trust-building, and conversion focus, not only visual taste.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No. However, Work could be moved earlier if proof is needed sooner.

## Section: About

### Primary Goal

Humanize the studio and explain who is behind the work.

### User Question It Answers

"Who will I be working with, and can I trust their judgment?"

### Business Goal

Build personal trust and reinforce the studio's combined skill set.

### Why This Section Exists

For service businesses, founder credibility matters. This section turns the studio from an abstract brand into a person and practice.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Works best after proof or process, when the user has a practical reason to care who is behind the service.

### Priority

Nice to have, bordering on essential for a founder-led studio.

### Could It Be Merged?

Yes. It could merge with Footer, Final CTA, or a compact trust panel after Work. If retained as a full section, it must add personal credibility that the rest of the page cannot provide.

## Section: FAQ

### Primary Goal

Resolve practical objections before the final conversion point.

### User Question It Answers

"What do I need to know before I reach out?"

### Business Goal

Reduce repetitive pre-sale questions and increase qualified inquiries.

### Why This Section Exists

Visitors may hesitate because of timeline, scope, copywriting, development responsibility, mobile behavior, budget, or fit. FAQ handles these concerns without requiring a sales call.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Services, Process, and About to provide context. It should answer questions raised by those sections.

### Priority

Essential if the studio receives recurring objections. Nice to have if the homepage must be very concise.

### Could It Be Merged?

Partially. A shorter FAQ could merge into Process or Final CTA. A full FAQ should remain separate only if it meaningfully reduces friction.

## Section: Final CTA

### Primary Goal

Convert accumulated understanding and trust into a direct conversation.

### User Question It Answers

"What should I do now?"

### Business Goal

Drive WhatsApp inquiries and move qualified visitors into discovery.

### Why This Section Exists

After the page has built the case, the final CTA gives a strong, focused endpoint.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on the entire page. It should summarize the decision in the user's mind, not introduce new complexity.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No. The final conversion moment should remain distinct, though it can be visually connected to the footer.

## Section: Footer

### Primary Goal

Provide brand closure, secondary navigation, and a final persistent contact path.

### User Question It Answers

"How do I get back to key sections or contact the studio from the bottom?"

### Business Goal

Capture users who scroll to the end and support basic site usability.

### Why This Section Exists

Long pages need a clear endpoint. The footer also supports navigation recovery.

### Dependencies On Previous Sections

Depends on Final CTA for conversion energy, then provides utility and closure.

### Priority

Essential.

### Could It Be Merged?

No, though it should visually follow the Final CTA calmly rather than compete with it.

## Critical Review

### What Should Remain

- Hero should remain. It is the clearest positioning anchor.
- Services should remain. Users need to understand what can be purchased.
- Process should remain. It reduces uncertainty around a custom service.
- Work should remain. Proof is essential for a premium studio.
- FAQ should remain in some form. It handles important buying objections.
- Final CTA and Footer should remain. They provide conversion and closure.

### What Should Be Merged

- Decision Board and Problem should be considered for merger. They both orbit the same decision logic: message, trust, proof, and action. A combined section could show "what fails" and "what must be built" in one tighter narrative.
- Approach and Process must be clearly separated or partially merged. Approach is philosophy; Process is execution. If the difference is not obvious in the UI, users will experience repetition.
- Story Scrolling could merge into Services as a featured service or into Work as a proof-led capability. As a standalone section, it earns its place only if this is a flagship differentiator.

### What Should Be Removed Or Reduced

- Remove any section that repeats the idea "strategy before design" without adding a new user decision. The current page risks over-explaining the same positioning.
- Reduce About if it remains generic. Founder-led trust matters, but the section should either add personal credibility, process confidence, or decision reassurance.
- Reduce decorative or conceptual sections if they slow comprehension on mobile.

### What May Be Missing

- A stronger proof layer may be needed before the Work section. This could be client outcomes, project metrics, testimonials, or a concise trust strip. Current proof relies mainly on portfolio examples.
- Pricing guidance or budget qualification may be missing. Even a range or "best fit for" framing could reduce unqualified inquiries.
- A clearer "who this is for" section may be useful. FAQ currently includes fit, but it appears late.
- A compact engagement model may be missing: what the client provides, what Velora owns, what happens after launch.

### Recommended Structural Direction

The strongest homepage would likely use this order:

1. Header
2. Hero
3. Problem plus Decision Framework
4. Services
5. Work or Proof
6. Process
7. Story Scrolling, only if treated as flagship differentiation
8. About, compact
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA
11. Footer

The biggest architectural question is whether the homepage should be a broad studio sales page or a flagship Story Scrolling sales page. If Story Scrolling is the flagship, it deserves more prominence and proof. If it is one capability among many, the standalone section should be shortened or merged.
